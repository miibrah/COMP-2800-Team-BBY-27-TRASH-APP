Why Recycle?

Reduce Landfills

In the UK, a quater of their methane emmisions are produced by their landfills. Recycled materials can be repurposed, thereby reducing the ammount of rubbish sent to landfills.

Save Energy

Producing new products from raw materials requires considerably more energy then if produced using recycled materials. Furthermore, extracting, refining, transporting, and proccessing new materials requires alot more work then using recycled industy ready materials.

Protects the planet

By recycling, we reduce our extracting, refining, and processing of resources (from quaries, mines, and forests), all of which produce water and air polution.

Rule of thumb's for Proper Recycling

Clean bottles, cans, cardbaord, and paper are recyclable.

Keep liquids and food away from your recycling. -----------

No bagged recyclables or loose plastic bags. -----------

Non-recyclable items
  • no packaging with multimaterials/mixed-plastics
  • no plastic or polystyrene foam packaging
  • no cups with plastic or wax coatings
  • no plastic wrap/film
Recycling Misconceptions
  1. Containers must be thoroughly cleaned to be recycled.

    2. False. The objective is to clean your recyclables enough where they do not contaminate other materials (i.e. your recycling bin, or paper).

  2. Seperating trash from your recyclables isn't that important.

    3. False. Putting non-recyclable items in your blue bin can cause costly damange to the equipment once it arrives at a recycling center. If you aren't sure what can be recycled or not, check your local recycling center.

  3. I can't recycle my delivery pizza boxes.

    4. It depends.You be the judge; if your pizza box isn't that greasy, and is free from leftovers or liners, it CAN be recycled.

  4. What you recycle must be in good condition

    5. False. Most of your recycled items will be compressed into pellets or melted anyways, so it being in imperfect condition does not really matter. However, broken glass is an exception. Be sure to wrap the boken shards into in a newspaper or place them inside a cardboard box before placing them inside the designated glass recycling bin (grey colored).
