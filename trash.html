Impacts of Mismanaged Trash

Trash can travel through the world via rivers and oceans, accumulating within marine ecosystems. This debris harms physical habitats, transports chemical pollutants, threatens aquatic life, and interferes with human uses of river, marine and coastal environments. Of all trash, plastic trash has the greatest potential to harm the environment, humans, and wild life. Plastic is transported to the oceans by rivers, where it moves with the currents and is eaten by fish and birds. This results in toxic chemicals building in their tissues, and filling their stomachs, causing them to starve. The effects of plastic waste cannot be understated.


Habitat Impacts

As debris (like plastic) accumulates in the ocean, habitat structure may be modified, light levels may be reduced in underlying waters, and oxygen levels may be depleted. These changes can undermine the ability of open water and sea-floor habitats to support aquatic life. Degradation of coral reefs due to the modification of their physical habitat has the potential to undermine the survival of a diverse array of invertebrates, fish, and vertebrates that depend on this limited resource, including many threatened and endangered species. This can cause a chain reaction destroying many marine ecosystems.


Chemical Impacts

The abundance of plastic in the ocean can result in bioaccumulation, which is the gradual accumulation of substances such as pesticides and other harmful chemicals in an organism. Typically, these chemicals are resistant to degradation and highly toxic. These chemicals have the tendency to biomagnify - which means that organisms higher up the food chain show higher levels of these toxic substances because they are constantly eating prey which has these chemicals embedded in their tissue. First, little creatures like phytoplankton are affected, but then when a zooplankton eats 10 phytoplankton, the pollution embedded in the zooplankton increases 10 fold! Then, a perch eats 10 zooplankton; now the perch has 100 times the toxic pollution as the inital phytoplankton! This bioaccumulation of toxic contaminants in organisms disrupts the food chain and can also poison humans.


Human Impacts

If you weren't convinced by the impact it has on marine life, hopefully you'll be convinced by the impact it has on you! Trash can directly interfere with navigation, impede commercial and recreational fishing, destroy soil quality, threaten health and safety, and reduce tourism. Overflowing garbage can result in air pollution, which can cause respiratory diseases and other negative health effects as contaminants are absorbed into the lungs.

How can I help?

A lot of times we dispose of items in the trash because we are either lazy or uninformed. We hope the above details have convinced you to stop being lazy. And if you're uninformed, that's okay! We are here to help.


Here are items that belong in the trash:

  • Rinsed diapers
  • Potato chip bags
  • Ziplock bags
  • Bubble wrap
  • Candy wrappers
/

Here are items that DO NOT belong in the trash:

  • Food scraps
  • Yard waste and leaves
  • Recyclable Items
  • Hazardous materials
  • Excrement
